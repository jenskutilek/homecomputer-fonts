The Homecomputer Fonts are inspired by the interface fonts of two classic 1980s computers, the Commodore C64 (Sixtyfour) and Amiga (Workbench). Jens Kutilek adapted them the the variable font technology. He did not just convert the pixel fonts, but tried to emulate the artifacts of rendering letters on a CRT screen. The width of the CRT scan lines and the horizontal bleed of the pixels can be controlled via variation axes.

The source files are available on GitHub.
